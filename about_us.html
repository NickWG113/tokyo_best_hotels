About this site

Luxury hotel picks in Tokyo. Compare rates and book when you're ready.

What we do

Tokyo Best Hotels is a simple guide to luxury stays in the city. We focus on properties that are worth the splurge: full-service five-star hotels, well-run boutique addresses, and a few places that do something you cannot really get elsewhere, like a ryokan-style stay in the middle of Otemachi.

Each listing includes the basics you need to narrow things down: neighbourhood, guest ratings, sample nightly rates, and a short note on what makes the hotel stand out. When you are ready to book, you can view the offer and confirm the price for your dates.

We are not a travel agency and we do not inspect rooms on your behalf. Think of this site as a starting point for planning, not the final word on every detail of your trip.

How we pick hotels

The list is short on purpose. We would rather show ten hotels we would actually recommend than fifty names you have to sift through. Most of what you will see here sits at the top end of the market: large rooms by Tokyo standards, strong service, good locations for first-time visitors and repeat travellers alike.

Some made the cut because of where they sit in the city. Otemachi and Marunouchi put you near Tokyo Station and the Imperial Palace. Nihonbashi and Toranomon work well if you want a central base without staying in Shinjuku or Shibuya. Yoyogi and Shiodome suit travellers who prefer a quieter pocket or a bay-side view.

We also pay attention to what guests say. High review scores matter, but so does consistency: clean rooms, helpful front desk staff, and hotels that still feel well run years after opening.

Why Tokyo

Tokyo is one of the few cities where luxury hotels feel genuinely different from one another. You can stay in a minimalist tower overlooking the palace gardens, a classic Japanese hotel that reopened after a full rebuild, or a small design hotel with a rooftop pool facing Yoyogi Park. The room sizes, breakfast styles, and even the bath setups vary more than in most capitals.

Service is a big part of it. Even at the big international brands, staff tend to be attentive without being stiff. Many hotels run serious restaurants in-house, and several on our list have spa floors, indoor pools, or onsen-style baths that are rare for a city hotel anywhere else.

If you are visiting for a few nights or a full week, picking the right neighbourhood matters as much as picking the brand. That is why we group each hotel by area and call out practical details like subway access, views, and what type of traveller the property suits best.

Planning your stay

Rates move with season, room type, and how far ahead you book. The prices on this site are a starting point. Always check the final total for your dates before you commit, especially around cherry blossom season, Golden Week, and the year-end holidays when Tokyo hotels fill up fast.

Most luxury hotels in Tokyo check in from 3pm and check out by noon. If you need an early arrival or late departure, it is worth asking the hotel directly. Breakfast is often optional and can be Japanese, Western, or a mix depending on the property.

When in doubt, start with the full list on our Best Hotels page. You can compare Aman Tokyo, The Peninsula, Mandarin Oriental, and the rest side by side, then open an offer when one fits your trip.
